üåê Lesson 1: What is a Network?
The Big Picture
Imagine you're the mayor of a small town. Your residents (devices) need to communicate with each other and with neighboring towns (other networks). You need to build roads (cables), traffic systems (protocols), and postal services (data transmission) to make this happen. That's essentially what a network is!

Core Concepts
What is a Network? A network is simply two or more devices connected together to share resources and communicate. Just like how you might share tools with your neighbors, computers share files, internet connections, and services.

Why Do We Need Networks?

Resource Sharing: Like sharing a community printer instead of everyone buying their own
Communication: Like having a phone system in an office building
Cost Efficiency: One internet connection for the whole house instead of separate connections for each device
Types of Networks (Size Matters!)
1. Personal Area Network (PAN)

Analogy: Your personal bubble - about arm's length
Example: Bluetooth connecting your phone to wireless earbuds
Range: About 10 feet
2. Local Area Network (LAN)

Analogy: Your neighborhood
Example: All devices in your home or office building
Range: Up to a few miles
3. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

Analogy: Your entire city
Example: City-wide Wi-Fi or cable TV network
Range: A city or large campus
4. Wide Area Network (WAN)

Analogy: The highway system connecting cities
Example: The Internet, or a company connecting offices in different states
Range: Unlimited - can span the globe
üè† Hands-On Exercise: Explore Your Home Network
What You'll Need:

Your computer/smartphone
Access to your Wi-Fi settings
Steps:

Find Your Network Name: Look at your Wi-Fi settings - that name is your network's "SSID"
Count Your Devices: How many devices are connected? Phone, laptop, smart TV, gaming console?
Find Your Router: That box with blinking lights is your network's "central hub"
Questions to Ask Yourself:

What type of network is your home network? (Hint: It's a LAN!)
How does your LAN connect to the outside world? (Through your internet provider's WAN)
üß† Key Terms Glossary
Term	Simple Definition	Real-World Analogy
Network	Connected devices that can communicate	A neighborhood with roads connecting houses
Node	Any device on a network	A house in the neighborhood
Host	A device that provides services	A house that runs a business (like a corner store)
Client	A device that uses services	A house that shops at the corner store
Server	A computer that provides resources/services	The corner store that serves the neighborhood
Protocol	Rules for how devices communicate	Traffic laws that everyone follows
Bandwidth	How much data can flow at once	How many cars can fit on a road
Latency	Delay in communication	How long it takes to drive across town
